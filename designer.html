Game Designer

Game designers create the games' concepts, characters, setting, story and game play. They collaborate with game artists and programmers to design the scripting language and visuals of the game.

Career Requirements

Degree Level High school diploma or equivalent; bachelor's preferred
Degree Field(s) Game design, computer engineering, or computer science
License/Certification Certification in programming languages and certificates available; Voluntary certifications available
Experience Sample projects typically required; Professional portfolio; Internships or co-ops
Skills Creativity; Knowledge of computer systems and languages; communication, collaboration, and project management skills; ability to tell a story and work in a team; knowledge of programming languages, software, and 3D modeling programs; ability to work many hours seated and looking at a computer monitor; passion and experience in video gaming
Job Outlook (2014-2024) (2015) faster than average growth
Avergage Annual Salary (2015) $98,260 (for application software developers)

Other Descriptions


While the minimum requirement to be a game designer is only a high school diploma, designers may need to get their bachelor's degree, especailly if they are planning to work for big gaming companies. They may need to have some experience working in computer science, programming or art to create the visuals as planned.

Game designers would also need to tell a story, work as a team and work for many hours seated, looking at a computer monitor all day and night. Additionally, they would need to have common knowledge of programming languages, software, and 3D modeling programs and a passion and experience in video gaming.

How it connects to CS?

Game designers use computer science because they have to stick to the limitations of the hardware. They need to make sure that whatever they design is possible for the rest of the team to make. If the designs does not work, they would have to find an alternative of how they can change their design so it falls within the limitations of the hardware without changing it drastically from the original idea.
