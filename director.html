Creative Game Director

Game directors are the creative brains behind the video game. Their job is to design the details of the video game. Similar to film directors, they make thier vision of the game into virual reality.

Career Requirements

Degree Level Bachelor's degree
Degree Field(s) Visual and Technical Arts; Graphic design, marketing, or a related field
Experience 7-10 years of experience
Skills Creativity; Keen-eye for Details; Logical and Organize Thinking; Interpersonal and Managerial Expertise; familiarity with graphic design and publication design software
Salary Range $42,000-$118,000
$83,429 (2016 median for creative directors)

Other Descriptions


Some of their tasks are between the game producers and the game designers. But their work load all depends on how big the gaming company is and what kind of project the person is currently working on. If one is working for a small company or with a small project, the director might take the position(s) of the designer, director, and/or producer for that one project.

Nevertheless, game directors usually manage the design team, act as the creativity designer, and create the grand-scale design trajectories. Some other responsibilities involve consulting with video game publishers, leading the team meetings, and hiring game developers.

Overall, the main purpose of the job is to check all the aspects of the game development, from start to finish with the director's perfect vision of the final product.

How it connects to CS?

Game director use computer science in thier work by understanding and solving any technical problems that can occur. They would need to understand the situation and possibly even some coding programs in order to communicate with the whole team. Just like any kind of director, they would need great communication and knowledge of the whole gaming production in order to create their vision of the video game.
